What is the "Danger Zone" temperature range for food?
The Danger Zone is between **40°F and 140°F** (4°C - 60°C). Bacteria grow most rapidly in this range.

---

What is the minimum internal temperature for cooking chicken?
You must cook chicken to at least **165°F (74°C)** to kill Salmonella.

---

What is cross-contamination?
It is the transfer of harmful bacteria to food from other foods, cutting boards, or utensils (e.g., using the same knife for raw meat and lettuce).

---

How should you thaw frozen meat safely?
Thaw it in the **refrigerator**, in cold water (changed every 30 mins), or in the microwave. Never thaw on the counter.
